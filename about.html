Our Story

Born from the Atlas Mountains, built for the world's explorers.

Meet Your Guide

Abdelaziz Aznag — Native Imazighen Guide

I was born in a small Berber village in the foothills of the High Atlas Mountains. Growing up, I spoke Tamazight — the ancient language of the Imazighen people — before I learned Arabic, French, and English. My childhood was spent between rocky mountain trails, terraced garden villages, and the stories of my grandparents about our people's 4,000-year history in these mountains.

After years guiding travelers from across the world, I realized that what people really wanted wasn't just to see Morocco — they wanted to feel it. To sit in a family home and share tea. To ride a camel through a valley that has barely changed in centuries. To look up at snow-capped peaks and understand, truly understand, that this land is alive.

That's why I founded Atlas Pathway Journeys — to share my home, my culture, and my people's story with anyone who is ready to listen. When you come on one of my tours, you're not a tourist. You're a welcomed guest.

"I want every person who travels with me to leave Morocco with more than photos — I want them to leave with a piece of the Atlas Mountains in their heart."

— Abdelaziz Aznag, Founder
