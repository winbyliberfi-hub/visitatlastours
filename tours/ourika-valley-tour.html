🏔️ About This Experience

Just one hour south of Marrakech, the Ourika Valley is a world away from the city's chaos. Nestled deep in the Atlas Mountains, this lush green valley — carved by the Ourika River — has been home to Berber communities for thousands of years.

Unlike the drier landscapes elsewhere in Morocco, the Ourika Valley is surprisingly green and fertile, with terraced gardens, rushing rivers, walnut trees, and colorful village houses stacked dramatically on rocky hillsides. The valley feels like stepping into another world — or another century.

Your guide Abdulaziz knows these mountains intimately. He'll take you to waterfalls that most tourists never find, introduce you to village elders who remember when the valley had no roads, and arrange an authentic mint tea ceremony in a local Berber home — the same hospitality his ancestors have offered travelers for generations.

This is Morocco's best-kept secret, and it's waiting for you. Best value day trip from Marrakech at just €35 per person.

📍 Itinerary

9:00 AM
Hotel Pickup
Comfortable pickup from your Marrakech hotel. The drive south takes approximately 1 hour through beautiful countryside landscapes as the Atlas Mountains grow larger on the horizon.
10:00 AM
Ourika Valley Arrival & First Views
Arrive at the valley entrance and get your first breathtaking view of the river valley with mountains rising on all sides. Walk along the riverbank, spot the traditional mud-brick villages clinging to the cliffs above, and breathe the pure mountain air.
10:30 AM
Waterfall Hike
Trek up to the famous Ourika waterfalls — a series of cascading falls hidden in the mountains. The hike takes approximately 45 minutes each way on a well-maintained path through stunning scenery. Abdulaziz shares stories of the valley along the way.
12:00 PM
Berber Village Visit & Mint Tea
Visit an authentic Berber village where Abdulaziz's community still lives. Be welcomed into a home for the famous Moroccan mint tea ceremony — tea poured from great height, sweet and fragrant. Meet the family, learn about daily life, and feel like a true guest rather than a tourist.
1:00 PM
Traditional Berber Lunch
Enjoy a delicious traditional Berber lunch overlooking the valley — tagine with seasonal vegetables, fresh salads, Moroccan bread, and mint tea. The restaurant is locally owned, and every meal supports the valley community.
2:30 PM
Argan Oil Cooperative
Stop at a traditional women's argan oil cooperative. Watch the ancient process of extracting Morocco's famous "liquid gold" by hand, learn about its uses, and support local women's empowerment through your purchase.
4:00–5:00 PM
Return to Marrakech
Scenic drive back along the valley road as afternoon light bathes the mountains in golden hues. Arrive back in Marrakech refreshed and inspired.

❓ FAQ

How difficult is the waterfall hike?

The hike to the Ourika waterfalls takes about 45 minutes each way on a well-maintained path. It involves some climbing on stone steps, but is suitable for anyone in reasonable health. Good walking shoes are recommended. Abdulaziz will set a pace that works for everyone in the group.

Is this tour suitable for elderly travelers?

For travelers with limited mobility, we can skip the waterfall hike and focus on the village visit, lunch, and valley exploration from accessible viewpoints. Please contact us in advance and we'll design the day around your needs.

What's the best time of year to visit Ourika Valley?

The Ourika Valley is beautiful year-round. Spring (March-May) is spectacular with wildflowers and the waterfalls at full flow. Summer is great for swimming in the river. Autumn offers golden foliage. Winter can see snow on the high peaks, creating stunning views.
