🌙 About This Magical Evening

As the sun dips below the Agafay Desert horizon, a magical evening unfolds. This is not just a dinner — it's an immersive Moroccan cultural experience that engages all your senses simultaneously and creates memories you'll treasure for the rest of your life.

Your evening begins as you leave Marrakech at sunset, arriving at the Agafay Desert camp just as the golden hour transforms the landscape into something otherworldly. The first moment — mounting your camel as the sky erupts in oranges, purples, and pinks — is one of the most photographed experiences in all of Morocco.

After the camel ride, sink into comfortable cushioned seats in the beautifully decorated Berber tent for a feast of authentic Moroccan dishes. Then, as darkness falls and a billion stars emerge above the desert, the entertainment begins — haunting Berber music, hypnotic Gnawa rhythms, and a breathtaking fire show performed by skilled local artists.

Finally, as the performers rest, the camp falls silent except for the desert wind. Look up. The Agafay sky — unpolluted by city lights — offers some of the most spectacular stargazing in all Morocco. You will return to Marrakech transformed.

📍 Evening Itinerary

6:00 PM
Hotel Pickup at Sunset
Comfortable transfer from your hotel as the evening glow begins. The 40-minute drive through the Marrakech outskirts is magical at sunset — watch the pink-tinged mountains appear on the horizon.
6:45 PM
Arrive & Sunset Camel Ride
Arrive at the desert camp and immediately mount your camel for the famous sunset ride. The timing is perfect — the sky is exploding with color as you gently sway through the desert landscape. Best photographs of your entire trip, guaranteed.
7:30 PM
Welcome Ceremony & Mint Tea
Be welcomed into the beautifully decorated Berber tent with sweet mint tea poured from great height — the traditional Moroccan greeting. Meet your fellow guests, relax on comfortable cushioned banquettes, and soak in the atmosphere.
8:00 PM
Traditional Moroccan Dinner Feast
A spectacular multi-course dinner featuring: Moroccan salad mezze, freshly baked Berber bread, a choice of slow-cooked tagine (chicken with preserved lemon & olives, or lamb with prunes & almonds, or vegetarian), followed by sweet bastilla dessert and fresh fruit.
9:00 PM
Live Berber Music & Fire Show
The highlight of the evening. Local musicians perform traditional Berber songs and Gnawa music on traditional instruments — the guembri, krakeb, and bendir. Then the fire performers take over, whirling flaming torches in patterns that seem to defy both physics and gravity.
10:00 PM
Stargazing Under the Desert Sky
As the entertainment winds down, step outside the tent and look up. Without city light pollution, the Agafay Desert reveals a Milky Way so bright it takes your breath away. Our guide shares Berber constellation stories passed down through generations.
11:00 PM
Return to Marrakech
Transfer back to Marrakech under the stars. Drop-off at your hotel. You'll return with memories that will last forever.

❓ FAQ

Is this experience suitable for children?

Yes! Children love the camel ride, music, and fire show. The evening ends at 11 PM which might be late for very young children. We recommend for children 5+. Please mention children's ages when booking.

What should I wear for an evening in the desert?

Comfortable clothes that you don't mind getting dusty from the camel ride. Bring a warm layer — the desert cools significantly at night. Comfortable shoes for walking on sand. We recommend bringing a light jacket or shawl regardless of summer temperatures.

Can I combine with the Day Pass for a full day & evening?

Absolutely! A full Day Pass + Dinner combo is one of our most popular options. Spend the day by the pool, have lunch, then stay for the magical dinner and entertainment. Contact us via WhatsApp for special combination pricing.

What if it rains or is too cold?

The Berber tent is covered and the experience continues regardless of weather. In exceptional weather events, we offer full rescheduling. The fire show and music happen under the tent. Marrakech rarely experiences heavy rain in the evening.
